Evaluating RAG with RAGAS

RAGAS is a framework for evaluating retrieval-augmented generation pipelines without
relying on large amounts of human-labelled data. It uses LLMs to score the quality
of both the retrieval and the generation stages along several complementary metrics.

Faithfulness measures whether the claims made in the generated answer are supported
by the retrieved context. An answer is faithful when every statement it makes can be
inferred from the context; unsupported claims lower the faithfulness score. This is
the primary metric for detecting hallucinations.

Answer relevancy measures whether the answer actually addresses the user's question.
An answer can be faithful to the context yet still fail to answer what was asked;
answer relevancy penalises responses that are off-topic, incomplete, or evasive.

Context recall measures whether the retrieval step fetched all the information needed
to answer the question, comparing the retrieved context against the ground-truth
answer. Low context recall indicates the retriever is missing relevant chunks.

Context precision measures whether the retrieved chunks are actually relevant, and
whether the relevant ones are ranked near the top. Low context precision indicates
the retriever is returning noise.

Together these four metrics separate retrieval problems from generation problems. If
faithfulness is high but context recall is low, the retriever is the bottleneck; if
context is good but faithfulness is low, the generator is hallucinating.
