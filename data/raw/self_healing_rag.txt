Self-Healing RAG and the LLM-as-Judge Pattern

A self-healing RAG pipeline adds a feedback loop to the standard retrieve-generate
flow so the system can detect and recover from its own failures rather than emitting
a confident but wrong answer.

The core of the loop is a critique step that uses the LLM-as-judge pattern. In this
pattern a language model evaluates another model's output instead of a human
annotator. Here the judge reads the question, the retrieved context, and the
generated answer, and returns a verdict: grounded, hallucinated, or insufficient.

A grounded verdict means every claim in the answer is supported by the context, so
the answer is returned to the user. A hallucinated verdict means the answer asserts
facts not present in the context. An insufficient verdict means the context genuinely
lacks the information needed, in which case the system honestly says it does not know
rather than fabricating an answer.

When the verdict is hallucinated, the system rewrites the query to be more specific
and retries retrieval, hoping to surface better context on the second attempt. To
avoid infinite loops, the number of retries is capped, commonly at two. After the
retry budget is exhausted, the system falls back to an honest "I don't know" instead
of returning an answer it could not ground.

This design is inspired by research on Self-RAG and Corrective RAG (CRAG), which
showed that adding self-reflection and corrective retrieval steps significantly
reduces hallucination rates compared with naive, linear RAG pipelines.
